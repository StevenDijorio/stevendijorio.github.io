Detailed Information: Diagnostic Summary of PBG Alterations

Challenges in Diagnosis:

A significant diagnostic challenge in post-transplant liver biopsies is dissecting the relative contributions of co-existing pathologies (e.g., IRI, ACR, chronic rejection, CMV). PBG alterations (atrophy, inflammation) can be non-specific. Therefore, PBG findings must be integrated with other histological features (type/location of inflammation, vascular changes, viral inclusions) and the broader clinical context.[4, 6]

Immunohistochemistry (IHC) Utility:

CK7 highlights biliary epithelium (including PBGs) and assesses ductopenia.[6] For CMV, IHC for viral antigens or ISH/RNAscope for nucleic acids are key.[8, 24] Research markers for PBG stem cells (Sox9, Nanog, EpCAM) exist but are not routine.[13]
