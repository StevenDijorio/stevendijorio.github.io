Detailed Information: Allograft Rejection

Acute Cellular Rejection (ACR):

Primarily a T-lymphocyte mediated response targeting interlobular bile ducts and likely PBGs.[5, 4] Histology shows lymphocytic infiltrates and ductitis (epithelial damage).[4] PBGs within affected ducts likely suffer similar injury, impairing mucin secretion and regeneration.

Chronic (Ductopenic) Rejection:

An insidious, often irreversible injury evolving from persistent ACR.[6, 7] Key features are bile duct loss (ductopenia) and obliterative arteriopathy (foam cell lesions compromising blood supply).[6, 7] Ductopenia implies PBG loss, either from direct immune attack or secondary ischemia.[5, 6] Periductal fibrosis is common.[5, 19]

Diagnostic Aids & Implications:

CK7 immunohistochemistry confirms duct loss and reveals cholangiolar metaplasia.[6] PBG destruction in chronic rejection abrogates the BTSC niche, leading to irreversible graft damage.[20] Distinguishing direct immune vs. ischemic PBG damage can be challenging in advanced stages.[5, 4, 6, 7]
